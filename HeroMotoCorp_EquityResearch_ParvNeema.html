Investment Thesis

A duopoly incumbent pivoting toward premiumisation and EVs

Hero MotoCorp is the world's largest two-wheeler manufacturer by volume, commanding roughly 34–36% market share of domestic two-wheelers. For over two decades, the company has earned its dominant position through unmatched distribution (10,000+ touchpoints) and brand strength in the mass/commuter category. Our thesis rests on three pillars: (1) a resilient core commuter franchise that generates superior free cash flows, (2) a credible premiumisation play—driven by the 125cc Xtreme & Xpulse portfolio and the Premia superbike tie-up with Harley-Davidson and Jawa-owner zero motorcycles—and (3) early-stage EV optionality via the Vida EV platform and a strategic stake in Ather Energy.

The model projects revenue growing at ~11.4% CAGR from FY25–FY30E to ₹70,423 Cr, supported by 5.5–6.3% volume CAGR alongside steady realization growth of ~4.5–5.6% annually as product mix shifts upward. Our FCFF-based DCF (15-year explicit period, terminal growth 6.35%) yields an intrinsic value of ₹5,457/share. Blending this with a peer-relative valuation at 38.1x NTM P/E (TVS, Bajaj, Eicher median) produces a blended target price of ₹7,035, representing 28.5% upside from the CMP of ₹5,472.

Risk Analysis

Key risks to our thesis

Market Share Erosion
Intensifying competition from Bajaj and Honda in the commuter segment could compress volumes below our projections. Every 1% market share loss ≈ ₹600–800 Cr revenue impact.
EV Disruption
Faster-than-expected EV adoption could cannibalize ICE commuter sales before Hero's Vida platform achieves competitive cost parity. Vida volumes currently sub-scale.
Commodity Cost Pressure
Steel, aluminium, and copper form ~50–55% of material cost. A 10% commodity spike without price pass-through could compress EBIT margin by ~100–150 bps.
Rural Demand Weakness
Entry-segment (~10 lakh units domestically) is highly rural and monsoon-sensitive. A sub-normal monsoon creates near-term volume headwinds of 3–5%.
Premiumisation Surprise
If premium/EV mix surpasses our 1.6 lakh unit FY30E estimate, realization growth of 7–8% YoY could push EBIT margins above our 15% forecast.
Export Ramp Optionality
Brazil and Southeast Asia penetration, combined with global Harley collaboration, could add 5–8 lakh export units by FY30E — an upside not in our base case.
